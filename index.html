What is industrial diving?

Industrial divers perform a variety of tasks at industrial diving sites. Marine rescue (salvage of sunken ships and relocation of stranded ships), underwater pier installation, ship berthing facilities, basic pier and breakwater construction, thermal and nuclear power plant cooling facilities, oil tanker terminal facilities, port dredging and rock crushing, etc. are countless. Diving skills

The standard when classifying diving is diving technique. Diving techniques can be classified in various ways according to breathing medium, equipment used, operation method, and activity field. Diving technology is a field of marine technology (Sea Technology or Marine Technology) and refers to all technologies performed underwater. It means Underwater Technology or Subsea Technology. In Japan, the term underwater work technology is used, but in our concept, it can be understood as diving technology.

industrial diving equipment

The three main elements of industrial diving are equipment, personnel, and materials. Here, the equipment refers to a Surface Supplied Diving System, which continuously supplies gas to the diver's helmet underwater through a flexible and sturdy lifeline (hose) from a gas supply source (air or mixed gas) on board or on land. It refers to the method of supply. Although this method is limited in its range of action, it can receive an unlimited supply of breathing gas from the ground, allowing it to remain underwater for a long time and enabling communication between divers on the ground and underwater.

How to become an industrial diver?

To become an industrial diver as a career, you need a high school diploma or higher, but no special major is required. However, if you major in civil engineering, electrical/electronics, mechanical, or oceanography, you can indirectly receive help in functional areas. Industrial divers start out as assistants and divers, and after gaining experience, they can become full-time industrial divers. In order to work as an industrial diver, you must have national technical qualifications as a diving technician and diving industrial engineer. In addition, it is more advantageous to get a job if you have civil engineering-related qualifications, hydraulic equipment-related qualifications, welding-related qualifications, and explosives-related qualifications.
